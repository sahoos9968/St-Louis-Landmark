Gateway Arch National Park

The one and only, Gateway Arch, stands tall along the Mississippi River.

A highlight of St Louis, this monument is also it's own national park.

The "Gateway to the West" represents many important ideas and values.

Learn the Facts

  • This famous landmark was completed in 1965, and was designed by Eero Saarinen
  • The Gateway Arch is considered the "Gateway to the West"
  • The famous Lewis and Clark Expedition originated near here. Part of the
    structure is honoring the Westward Exploration.
  • The historic Dread Scott Case occured at the old Court House,
    which is also located in the Gateway Arch Natinal Park

How to Visit

    There are 3 places that you need to see on your trip
    to the Gateway Arch National Park!

  • At the bottom of the Arch lies the visitor's center. It's a great place to understand a bit
    more about the arch.
  • Next comes the arch itself! Take the cool elevator up there, and enjoy the views!
  • Do take some pictures of the old courthouse as well! It's on the national park property
    and is where the famous Dred Scott Case happened.
